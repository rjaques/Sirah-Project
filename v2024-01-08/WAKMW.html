ووجدنا ابن أبي داود قد حدثنا قال حدثنا @TR1_AKMW@ أحمد بن خالد الوهبي قال حدثنا محمد بن إسحاق عن عمه موسى بن يسار عن أبي هريرة عن النبي صلى الله عليه وسلم فذكر مثله غير أنه قال له في الثالثة أو الرابعة اركبها ويحك.

Muḥammad al-Ṭaḥāwī, Sharḥ mushkil al-āthār, vol. 8 p. 320) and SHBMV03P028G (Ibn Abī Shaybah, al-Kitāb al-muṣannaf fī al-aḥāddīth waʾl-āthār, vol. 3 p. 28); WMUTK (Muḥammad b. ʿUbayd b. Ibrāhīm b. ʿAbd al-Raḥmān al-Madinī al-Zuhrī): HBHXV39P362A (Aḥmad b. Ḥanbal, Musnad, vol. 39 p. 362) and KTHRV07P018B ([REFERENCE NOT FOUND IN BIBLIOGRAPHY], vol. 7 p. 18). Ibrāhīm al-Bukhārī, Taʾrīkh al-kabīr, vol. 2 p. 177), Abū ʿAlī Thumāmah b. Shufayy al-Hamdānī only related from a few people, including Faḍālah, ʿUqbah b. ʿĀmir, and Qubayṣah b. Dhuʾayb. He appears to have been regarded as one of the people of Egypt. RZJXV02P466C (Ibn Abī Ḥātim al-Rāzī, al-Jarḥ waʾl-taʿdīl, vol. 2 p. 466) states that Thumāmah related material to Ibn Isḥāq as well as Yazīd b. Abī Ḥabīb, the source of the only clarification tradition in the group found on in the WAKMW (Aḥmad b. Khālid b. Mūsā al-Wahbī al-Kindī) witness; all other the other traditions come to Ibn Isḥāq directly through Thumāmah. ʿAbd al-Raḥmān b. Aḥmad b. Yūnus al-Ṣadafī, in his history of Egypt, refers to him as Thumāmah b. Shufayy al-Hamdānī, as well as al-Uḥrūjī al-Miṣrī, although what “Uḥrūj” refers to in not known. al-Ṣadafī claims that Thumāmah participated in the raid on Rhodes (Rūdas) with Faḍālah b. ʿUbayd and that he died before 120/737 during the caliphate of Hishām b. ʿAbd al-Mālik (reign c. 724-743) (see TYNXV01P080A (Abū Saʿīd ʿAbd al-Raḥmān b. Aḥmad b. Yūnus al-Ṣadafī, Taʾrīkh Ibn Yūnus al-Miṣrī, vol. 1 p. 80)). al-Mizzī states that Thumāmah settled in Alexandria (MZTXV04P404A (al-Mizzī, Tahdhīb al-kamāl fī asmāʾ al-rijāl, vol. 4 p. 404)). Abī Umayyah ʿAbd al-Raḥmān al-Īyādī al-Ṭanāfisī al-Kūfī) (who were brothers and likely heard the story at the same time), state that the event took place in the time of Muʿāwiyah during the invasion of “Rūm” (see SHBMV03P024L (Ibn Abī Shaybah, al-Kitāb al-muṣannaf fī al-aḥāddīth waʾl-āthār, vol. 3 p. 24) and HBHXV39P359B (Aḥmad b. Ḥanbal, Musnad, vol. 39 p. 359) respectively). WSACD (Ibrāhīm b. Saʿd b. Ibrāhīm b. ʿAbd al-Raḥmān al-Madinī al-Zuhrī) also states in one of the traditions he witnessed that it occurred during the invasion of “Rūm” but does not place it in a time period (see HBHXV39P362A (Aḥmad b. Ḥanbal, Musnad, vol. 39 p. 362)). According to Khalīfah b. Khayyāṭ, the invasion of Rhodes occurred in 59/678-679, the last year of Muʿāwīyah’s reign, under the leadership of Junādah b. Abī Umayyah (see SFTXV01P227B (Khalīfah b. Khayyāṭ al-Shaybānī al-ʿAṣfarī, Taʾrīkh, vol. 1 p. 227)). There is, however, some dispute about the year that Faḍālah b. ʿUbayd died, some placing his death in 53, others in 67. Ibn Kathīr, quoting Ibn al-Jawzī, states that Faḍālah died in 55, apparently just before the capture of Rhodes, which he covers in the following section (see KTHBV08P078B (Ibn Kathīr, al-Bidāyat waʾl-nihāyah, vol. 8 p. 78)). If this is accurate, the tradition may have had a foreshadowing element since it talks about death and burial just before Faḍālah’s own death. Placement should be in the year 55 or 56 under the heading of the Invasion of Rhodes.

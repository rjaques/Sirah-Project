السيرة النبوية لمحمد بن اسحاق في رواية عمر بن حبيب القاضي



DRQSV02P097A

al-Dāraquṭnī, Sunan al-Dāraquṭnī, vol. 2 p. 97

حدثنا يوسف بن يعقوب بن إسحاق بن بهلول ثنا أحمد بن علي العمي ثنا عمر بن حبيب القاضي ثنا محمد بن إسحاق بهذا الإسناد نحوه وقال كأنكم تقرءون خلفي قلنا أجل هذا يا رسول الله قال فلا تفعلوا إلا بفاتحة الكتاب فإنه لا صلاة إلا بها


THSMV01P262G

Aḥmad b. Muḥammad al-Ṭaḥāwī, Sharḥ maʿānī al-āthār, vol. 1 p. 262

حدثنا ابن مرزوق، قال: ثنا عمر بن حبيب، قال: ثنا محمد بن إسحاق، عن عبد الرحمن بن الأسود، عن أبيه، عن عبد الله، أنه قال: «أخذت التشهد من في رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم ولقننيها كلمة كلمة» ثم ذكر التشهد الذي في حديث أبي وائل وزاد قال: «فكانوا يخفون التشهد ولا يظهرونه»

DRQSV02P099A

al-Dāraquṭnī, Sunan al-Dāraquṭnī, vol. 2 p. 99

حدثنا يوسف بن يعقوب بن إسحاق بن بهلول ثنا أحمد بن علي العمي ثنا @TR1_UMHA@ عمر بن حبيب القاضي ثنا محمد بن إسحاق بهذا الإسناد نحوه وقال كأنكم تقرءون خلفي قلنا أجل هذا يا رسول الله قال فلا تفعلوا إلا بفاتحة الكتاب فإنه لا صلاة إلا بها.

COMMENT

It is not found in the redaction of Ibn Hishām, nor is the phrase "Umm al-Qur'an." This represents one of Ibn Isḥāq's most frequently reused passages. COMMENT

According to Khaṭīb al-Baghdādī (d. 463), he was one of the memorizers of ḥadīth who traveled in search of new traditions. Ibn Isḥāq is not listed in the Taʾrīkh Baghdād as relating to him. He was born in 228 and began to write traditions down at in the year 239 (KHTGV16P341A (al-Khaṭīb al-Baghdādī, Taʾrīkh Baghdād, vol. 16 p. 341)), writing 5 or 6 volumes. Since Ibn Isḥāq died at the latest in 159 there is no way Yaḥyā b. Ṣāʿid could have related directly from him. Where listed as relating from Ibn Isḥāq, there must be a link or two missing. He is not listed by Khaṭīb as relating from any of Ibn Isḥāq’s witnesses. According to Ibn ʿAsākir, he did hear from Saʿīd b. Yaḥyā b. Saʿīd al-Umawī, the son of the witness Yaḥyā b. Saʿīd al-Umawī (WYSAU (Yaḥyā b. Saʿīd b. Abān al-Qurashī al-Umawī al-Kūfī)) in Iraq (TDSHV64P357A (Ibn ʿAsākir, Taʾrīkh madīnat Dimashq, vol. 64 p. 357)). There is reason to think that the Ibn Isḥāq listed in HJRTV06P424B (Ibn Ḥajar al-ʿAsqalānī, Itḥāf al-maharah bi-al-fawāʾid al-mubtakarah min aṭrāf al-ʿasharah, vol. 6 p. 424) is actually Muḥammad b. Isḥāq b. Khuzaymah (see, for instance, TLBXV07P3373B (Ibn al-ʿAdīm, Bughyat al-ṭalab fī taʾrīkh Ḥalab, vol. 7 p. 3373)). In the isnād found in HJRTV06P424B (Ibn Ḥajar al-ʿAsqalānī, Itḥāf al-maharah bi-al-fawāʾid al-mubtakarah min aṭrāf al-ʿasharah, vol. 6 p. 424) that lists Ibn Isḥāq as relating to Yaḥyā b. Ṣāʿid, Ibn Isḥāq’s source was ʿAbd Allāh b. Yūsuf al-Tunīsī, who was not one of our Ibn Isḥāq’s sources. The other possible source listed, is Ibn Abī Farwah, or Isḥāq b. Abī Farwah who could be one of several individuals, is not listed anywhere as a source for Ibn Isḥāq. In the above passage, and also in DRQSV02P099C (al-Dāraquṭnī, Sunan al-Dāraquṭnī, vol. 2 p. 99), BQSXV02P234C (al-Bayhaqī, Sunan al-kubrā, vol. 2 p. 234), and BQQXV01P056C (al-Bayhaqī, Kitāb al-qurāʾ khalaf al-imām, vol. 1 p. 56), Ibn Ṣāʿid is listed as relating from ʿUbayd Allāh b. Saʿd, the grandson of WSACD (Ibrāhīm b. Saʿd b. Ibrāhīm b. ʿAbd al-Raḥmān al-Madinī al-Zuhrī). It is therefore possible that there are several links between Ibn Isḥāq and Ibn Ṣāʿid. It is this lack of clarity that Ibn Ḥajar al-ʿAsqalānī seeks to clarify.

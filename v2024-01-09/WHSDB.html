THSHV04P379A

Aḥmad b. Muḥammad al-Ṭaḥāwī, Sharḥ mushkil al-āthār, vol. 4 p. 379

حدثنا الحسين بن نصر قال حدثنا يحيى بن حسان وحدثنا بكار بن قتيبة قال حدثنا روح بن عبادة وحدثنا محمد بن خزيمة قال حدثنا حجاج بن منهال قالوا جميعا حدثنا @TR1_HSDB@ حماد بن سلمة عن محمد بن إسحاق عن @EW1_YAHA@ يزيد بن أبي حبيب عن أبي مرزوق عن فضالة بن عبيد قال دعا رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم بشراب فقال له بعضنا ألم تصبح صائما يا رسول الله قال بلى ولكني قئت.

COMMENT

This is part of a series of traditions that appear to originate with Faḍālah b. ʿUbayd that discuss vomiting and its impact on the status of fasting. In this series there is a great deal of variation, but most suggest that Faḍālah was with the prophet and when someone offered him a drink or when he asked for a drink, Muḥammad was asked about the status of his fast. He replies generally that he was had been fasting but then vomited and was no longer fasting. In some traditions in the series there are comments about whether vomiting “breaks” the fast or if Muḥammad chose to end his fast after vomiting. There appears to be some dispute in the legal tradition about vomiting and its impact on the status of the fast, which these traditions address.
